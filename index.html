Dataset Overview

Read and Tell (RaT) is a multilingual speech corpus designed to support the development of robust, fair, and real-world-ready speech technologies. It contains recordings from 50 speakers, each contributing five minutes of read speech and five minutes of spontaneous speech, collected using different smartphones in controlled indoor environments.

The dataset is balanced by gender (25 female, 25 male) and diversified in terms of age, language, and nationality. Participants represent a wide range of linguistic backgrounds, with recordings available in multiple languages.

üöÄ Use Cases

Speaker Verification

Evaluate speaker verification systems under different speaking styles and assess robustness across read vs. spontaneous speech patterns.

Gender & Age Classification

Train and test voice-based demographic classification models with balanced representation across different age groups and genders.

Device Robustness

Evaluate speech model performance across different smartphone recording devices and assess cross-device generalization.

Style-Sensitive Models

Develop text-to-speech systems and speaker identification models that can distinguish between read and spontaneous speech styles.

Prosodic Analysis

Study prosodic and acoustic differences between scripted reading and natural spontaneous speech across multiple languages.

Multilingual Research

Conduct cross-lingual speech research and develop language-independent speech processing algorithms.
